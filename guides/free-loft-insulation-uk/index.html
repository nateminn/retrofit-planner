How to Get Free Loft Insulation in the UK in 2026

The ECO4 scheme provides free loft insulation to qualifying UK households until December 2026. If you receive means-tested benefits and your home has an EPC rating of D or below, you likely qualify. Here is exactly how to get it.

GBIS has closed

The Great British Insulation Scheme closed to new applications on January 31, 2026. ECO4 is now the main route to free insulation and remains open until December 2026. Apply through your energy supplier.

Who qualifies for free loft insulation?

You typically qualify for free loft insulation through ECO4 if you meet all of these criteria.

You receive a qualifying benefit. This includes Universal Credit, Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit), Child Tax Credit (income below £16,385), Income Support, income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, Housing Benefit, or Working Tax Credit (income below £16,385). If anyone in your household receives one of these benefits, the whole household qualifies.

Your home has an EPC rating of D, E, F, or G. Check your EPC rating for free at gov.uk/find-energy-certificate. If your home does not have an EPC, you can still apply and one will be arranged as part of the process.

Your loft has less than 100mm of insulation. If your loft already has 100mm or more, it is not eligible for a top-up through ECO4. The recommended depth is 270mm.

Your loft is accessible. The loft must have a hatch of at least 400mm (16 inches) and be largely unboarded. If more than 20% of the loft floor is boarded, the boards need to be lifted before installation (which ECO4 does not always cover).

What if you are not on benefits?

You may still qualify through ECO4 Flex, a route that allows local councils to refer households who do not receive qualifying benefits but are vulnerable to fuel poverty. You may be eligible if your household income is below £31,000 per year, you or someone in your household has a health condition worsened by living in a cold home (respiratory or cardiovascular conditions), or you live in a property with an EPC of E, F, or G and are in a low council tax band (A to D).

Contact your local council directly to ask if they operate an ECO4 Flex scheme. Not all councils participate, but many do.

How to apply (step by step)

Step 1: Check your EPC. Go to gov.uk/find-energy-certificate and search your address. If your rating is D or below, proceed. If you do not have an EPC, apply anyway and tell your supplier.

Step 2: Contact your energy supplier. Call or go online to your current electricity or gas supplier. The major suppliers obligated under ECO4 include British Gas, EDF, E.ON, Scottish Power, Octopus Energy, OVO, and Shell Energy. Tell them you want to apply for ECO4 loft insulation.

Step 3: Eligibility check. Your supplier will verify your benefits and EPC. This usually takes a few days. They may ask for proof of benefits.

Step 4: Home survey. A surveyor visits your home to confirm your loft is suitable. They check the existing insulation depth, loft access, ventilation, and any issues like damp or structural problems. This visit is free.

Step 5: Installation. A certified installer lays mineral wool insulation to 270mm depth across your loft floor. This typically takes 1 to 2 hours for a standard loft. You do not need to clear the loft completely, but large items near the hatch should be moved.

Step 6: Completion. The installer provides documentation and your EPC is updated to reflect the improvement. You should notice warmer rooms and lower heating bills within the first heating season.

The entire process from first contact to installation typically takes 4 to 8 weeks.

How much does loft insulation save?

Savings depend on your property type. Detached houses save the most because they have the largest roof area. These figures are from the Energy Saving Trust for homes on gas heating.

A detached house saves approximately £355 per year. A semi-detached saves £215. An end-terrace saves £175. A mid-terrace saves £135. A bungalow saves £280. A flat saves £100. Homes heated by oil save roughly 35% more. Homes on LPG save roughly 40% more.

Loft insulation lasts 40 years or more with no maintenance. Over its lifetime, free loft insulation in a semi-detached house saves approximately £8,600 at current energy prices. Use our insulation savings calculator for a personalised estimate.

What about the Great British Insulation Scheme?

The Great British Insulation Scheme (GBIS) was a separate government programme that ran alongside ECO4. It had broader eligibility, covering households in council tax bands A to D even without benefits. However, GBIS closed to new applications on January 31, 2026. Installations under existing GBIS applications will complete by March 31, 2026.

If you missed the GBIS deadline, ECO4 is now your primary route to free insulation. ECO4 runs until December 2026. The eligibility criteria are narrower (benefits required for the main route) but the ECO4 Flex option through your local council may still cover you.

What if your loft is converted?

If your loft has been converted into a room, standard loft floor insulation is not suitable. Instead, you may be eligible for room-in-roof insulation, which is fitted between and under the rafters. This is also available through ECO4 but is more expensive and complex to install. Tell your supplier about the conversion when you apply.

Should you insulate your loft before getting a heat pump?

Yes, absolutely. Heat pumps work best in well-insulated homes. Adding loft insulation before a heat pump installation means you need a smaller (cheaper) heat pump, the heat pump runs more efficiently, and your home stays warmer at lower flow temperatures. The best approach is to get free loft insulation through ECO4, then apply for the £7,500 BUS grant for a heat pump. That combination gives you two major upgrades for minimal personal cost.

Frequently asked questions

Can I get free loft insulation in 2026?

Yes, through the ECO4 scheme until December 2026. You need qualifying benefits and an EPC of D or below. Contact your energy supplier to apply. Check your full eligibility.

How do I apply for free loft insulation?

Contact your energy supplier directly (British Gas, EDF, E.ON, Scottish Power, Octopus, OVO, or Shell Energy). Ask to apply for ECO4 loft insulation. They handle the eligibility check, survey, and installation.

Can I get free loft insulation without being on benefits?

Potentially, through ECO4 Flex. Contact your local council to ask if they refer households based on income (below £31,000), health conditions, or fuel poverty indicators.

How much does free loft insulation save?

£135 to £355 per year on gas heating, depending on property type. More for oil or LPG heating. Loft insulation lasts 40+ years. Calculate your specific savings.
