Heat Pump vs New Boiler: Which Is Actually Cheaper in 2026?

Over 15 years, a heat pump in a well-insulated home costs £1,000 to £3,000 less than a gas boiler when you factor in the £7,500 grant, comparable running costs, lower maintenance, and longer lifespan. But in a poorly insulated home, the gas boiler still wins on running costs. Here are the numbers.

After the £7,500 BUS grant, the upfront gap is much smaller than people expect. For a 2 bed terrace, a heat pump can actually be cheaper upfront than a new boiler. For larger homes, the heat pump costs £1,000 to £3,000 more.

Running costs compared

At Ofgem Q1 2026 rates (electricity 24.5p/kWh, gas 6.76p/kWh), the effective cost per kWh of heat delivered is:

Gas boiler (92% efficient): 6.76p divided by 0.92 = 7.35p per kWh of heat.

Heat pump at COP 2.8 (average insulation): 24.5p divided by 2.8 = 8.75p per kWh of heat.

Heat pump at COP 3.2 (good insulation): 24.5p divided by 3.2 = 7.66p per kWh of heat.

Heat pump at COP 3.5 (excellent insulation): 24.5p divided by 3.5 = 7.00p per kWh of heat.

The crossover point is a COP of approximately 3.3. Above that, the heat pump is cheaper to run. Below it, gas wins on running costs. Your COP depends almost entirely on your insulation quality. A smart heat pump tariff can further reduce the effective electricity rate by 15% to 25%, tipping the balance further towards the heat pump.

Maintenance and lifespan

A gas boiler needs annual servicing at £80 to £120. It typically lasts 12 to 15 years. An air source heat pump needs servicing every 1 to 3 years at £100 to £200 per service. It typically lasts 20 to 25 years. The heat pump has lower annual maintenance costs and lasts significantly longer.

Over 15 years: gas boiler maintenance costs approximately £1,200 to £1,800. Heat pump maintenance costs approximately £750 to £1,500. The gas boiler may also need replacing within that period, while the heat pump almost certainly will not.

When the heat pump wins

The heat pump is the better choice when your home is well insulated (COP 3.0 or above), you are replacing an oil, LPG, or electric heating system (savings are much larger), you plan to stay in the property for 10+ years, you want to improve your EPC rating (heat pumps add 10 to 15 points), you plan to add solar panels (free electricity for the heat pump), or you want to future-proof against the gas boiler phase-out.

When the boiler wins

A new gas boiler may be the better choice when your home is poorly insulated and you cannot or will not improve it, you are selling the property within 5 years (shorter payback window), you need hot water at very high flow rates (combi boiler advantage), or the upfront cost difference is a genuine financial barrier even after the grant.

If insulation is the blocker, check whether you qualify for free insulation through ECO4. That changes the equation completely.

Why insulation decides the answer

In a well-insulated home, the heat pump wins on almost every measure: comparable running costs, lower lifetime costs, longer lifespan, better EPC, lower carbon. In a poorly insulated home, the gas boiler wins on running costs because the heat pump's COP drops and it has to work harder.

The smartest approach: insulate first (often free through grants), then install a heat pump. This gives you the best of both worlds: lower running costs than a gas boiler and a government grant covering most of the installation cost.

Frequently asked questions

Is a heat pump cheaper than a new boiler?

Over 15 years in a well-insulated home, yes. The £7,500 BUS grant closes the upfront gap, running costs are comparable, and the heat pump lasts 5 to 10 years longer. Run your own comparison.

Should I replace my boiler with a heat pump in 2026?

If your home is well insulated, yes. If not, insulate first. The £7,500 grant makes the upfront cost competitive. Calculate your costs.

How much cheaper is a heat pump to run than a gas boiler?

At current prices, running costs are similar in well-insulated homes. The heat pump becomes cheaper with a smart tariff or solar panels. For oil or LPG homes, heat pumps save 30% to 60%. See our running costs guide.

Will gas boilers be banned in the UK?

New-build homes are transitioning away from gas. For existing homes, policy is moving towards low-carbon heating but no firm ban date is set. A boiler installed today will likely need a low-carbon replacement within its 15-year lifespan.

What is the 15-year cost of a heat pump vs gas boiler?

For a well-insulated 3 bed semi: gas boiler approximately £14,875 total, heat pump approximately £14,500 total (after grant). The heat pump also has 5 to 10 years remaining lifespan. Run your own comparison.
