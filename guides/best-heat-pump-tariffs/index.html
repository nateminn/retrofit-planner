Best Heat Pump Tariffs UK 2026

Switching to a heat pump tariff can save 15% to 35% on your heat pump electricity costs. That is £100 to £350 per year for a typical home. The right tariff depends on your lifestyle and whether you have solar panels or a battery. Here is how the main options compare.

Octopus Cosy: best all-round heat pump tariff

Octopus Cosy is designed specifically for heat pump owners. It offers three daily "cosy" windows with reduced electricity rates, typically in the early morning, midday, and early evening. These windows align with when most homes run their heat pumps heaviest.

The weighted average electricity cost for heat pump usage comes to approximately 21p per kWh, compared to the 24.5p Ofgem cap. For a typical 3 bed semi using 3,000 kWh per year for heating, that saves roughly £105 per year.

Octopus Cosy requires a smart meter and a compatible heat pump with smart controls. Most modern heat pumps from Daikin, Vaillant, Samsung, and other major brands are compatible. You do not need to be an existing Octopus customer to switch.

Octopus Agile: highest savings for flexible users

Octopus Agile offers half-hourly pricing that tracks wholesale electricity costs. Rates can drop to 5p per kWh (or even negative) during overnight and low-demand periods, but spike to 30p to 35p during the 4pm to 7pm evening peak.

If you can shift most of your heat pump operation to off-peak hours (pre-heating your home in the early morning and midday when rates are lowest), Agile can save 25% to 35% on heat pump electricity. With a battery to store cheap electricity for peak hours, savings increase further.

The downside is unpredictability. Your rate changes every 30 minutes, and evening peak rates are higher than the standard cap. You need a smart meter, a willingness to engage with your energy usage, and ideally a home automation system or app that schedules your heat pump around the cheapest periods.

Economy 7 and Economy 10

Economy 7 offers a cheap overnight rate (roughly 12p per kWh for 7 hours, usually 11pm to 6am) and a higher daytime rate (roughly 28p per kWh). Economy 10 gives 10 cheap hours split between overnight and afternoon.

These tariffs can work for heat pumps if your home has good thermal mass (thick walls, concrete floors, or a large hot water cylinder) that retains heat absorbed overnight. You pre-heat the house and hot water during cheap hours and coast through the day on stored heat.

However, Economy 7 daytime rates are typically higher than the Ofgem cap, so if your heat pump runs during the day (which it will in cold weather), you lose money. For most heat pump owners, Octopus Cosy or Agile is a better choice because the cheap windows are distributed throughout the day.

Why the standard variable tariff is the worst option

If you have a heat pump and are still on your supplier's standard variable tariff at the full Ofgem cap rate of 24.5p per kWh, you are overpaying by 15% to 35%. Switching tariff takes minutes, costs nothing, and saves £100 to £350 per year. There is no reason not to switch. Combining tariffs with solar panels

The most cost-effective setup for a heat pump is a smart tariff plus solar panels. During the day, your heat pump runs on free solar electricity. During the morning and evening, it runs on cheap tariff electricity. With a battery, you can store solar and off-peak electricity to cover the evening peak entirely.

This combination can reduce heat pump running costs by 40% to 60% compared to a standard tariff without solar. For a 3 bed semi, that means annual heat pump electricity costs of £300 to £500 instead of £700 to £950. See our solar panel calculator for a personalised estimate.

How to switch tariffs

Switching is free and usually takes 2 to 3 weeks. You need a smart meter (if you do not have one, your supplier will install it for free). Visit the supplier's website and sign up for the tariff. Your existing supplier does not need to agree, and there are no exit fees from standard variable tariffs.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best electricity tariff for a heat pump?

Octopus Cosy is the best all-round choice, saving approximately 15% on heat pump electricity. Octopus Agile saves 25% to 35% but requires more flexibility.

How much can a smart tariff save on heat pump running costs?

£100 to £350 per year for a typical home. See our running costs guide for your baseline figures.

Should I get Economy 7 for a heat pump?

Probably not. Daytime rates are higher than standard. Octopus Cosy or Agile are better for most heat pump owners because they offer multiple cheap windows throughout the day.

Can I combine a heat pump tariff with solar panels?

Yes. This combination can reduce heat pump costs by 40% to 60%. Calculate your solar ROI.
