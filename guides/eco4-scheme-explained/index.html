ECO4 Scheme Explained: Who Qualifies and How to Apply

The ECO4 scheme provides free insulation and heating upgrades to qualifying UK households. If you receive means-tested benefits and your home has a low EPC rating, you could get loft insulation, cavity wall insulation, solid wall insulation, or a new heating system at no cost. The scheme runs until December 2026.

Apply soon

ECO4 runs until December 2026, but energy suppliers may reach their targets before the deadline. Applications are first-come, first-served. Do not wait until the last month.

What is the ECO4 scheme?

ECO4 stands for Energy Company Obligation, phase 4. It is a government scheme that legally requires large energy suppliers (those with more than 150,000 domestic customers) to fund energy efficiency improvements in eligible homes. The scheme started in April 2022 and runs until December 2026.

The obligated suppliers include British Gas, EDF, E.ON, Scottish Power, Octopus Energy, OVO Energy, and Shell Energy. These companies must meet government-set targets for improving the energy efficiency of qualifying homes. They fund the improvements entirely, meaning you pay nothing.

ECO4 specifically targets homes with low energy efficiency (EPC D to G) occupied by households receiving means-tested benefits. The goal is to improve these homes by at least two EPC bands where possible, and to at least Band C.

Who qualifies for ECO4?

The main eligibility route requires two things: you (or someone in your household) receive a qualifying benefit, and your home has an EPC rating of D, E, F, or G.

Qualifying benefits include: Universal Credit, Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit), Child Tax Credit (income below £16,385), Working Tax Credit (income below £16,385), Income Support, income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, Housing Benefit, and the Warm Home Discount (Core Group).

Property requirements: your home must have an EPC rating of D or below. Check yours free at gov.uk/find-energy-certificate. You can be a homeowner or a private tenant (with landlord's permission). Social housing is eligible but managed separately through social housing providers.

ECO4 Flex: qualifying without benefits

If you do not receive qualifying benefits, you may still qualify through ECO4 Flex. This route allows local councils to refer households that are vulnerable to fuel poverty. You may qualify if your household income is below £31,000 per year, you or someone in your household has a cardiovascular, respiratory, or other health condition worsened by cold, you live in a property with an EPC of E, F, or G, or your local council identifies you as fuel-poor based on local criteria.

Not all councils participate in ECO4 Flex. Contact your local council to ask. If they do participate, they will assess your situation and provide a referral letter that your energy supplier can use to process your application.

What does ECO4 cover?

ECO4 covers a wide range of energy efficiency measures. The specific improvements offered to you depend on your home survey results. Common measures include:

Insulation: loft insulation (from zero or top-up to 270mm), cavity wall insulation, solid wall insulation (internal or external), floor insulation, room-in-roof insulation, and flat roof insulation.

Heating: first-time central heating (for homes that have never had it), boiler replacements (in limited circumstances where the existing boiler is beyond repair), heating controls, and in some cases heat pumps (though the BUS grant is usually the better route for heat pumps).

Other measures: draught-proofing, window glazing (in limited circumstances), and ventilation improvements.

The scheme aims to improve your home by at least two EPC bands. Your surveyor will recommend the combination of measures that achieves the greatest improvement. You may receive multiple improvements in a single visit. Use our EPC improvement calculator to see which measures add the most points for your property.

How to apply (step by step)

Step 1: Check your EPC. Visit gov.uk/find-energy-certificate. If you have no EPC or it is rated D or below, proceed.

Step 2: Contact your energy supplier. Call your current gas or electricity supplier. Ask to apply for ECO4. You can also apply through a different obligated supplier if your current one is not participating or has reached its quota.

Step 3: Eligibility verification. The supplier checks your benefits status (often through the DWP database) and your EPC. This takes a few days to a few weeks.

Step 4: Home survey. A qualified surveyor visits your home to assess what measures are needed and suitable. This is free. The surveyor checks insulation levels, heating system, windows, and overall condition.

Step 5: Installation. Certified installers carry out the recommended improvements. Loft insulation takes 1 to 2 hours. Cavity wall insulation takes 2 to 3 hours. Solid wall insulation takes 1 to 2 weeks. You pay nothing.

Step 6: New EPC. After installation, a new EPC assessment is carried out to confirm the improvement. This is included at no cost.

Timeline from application to installation

Expect 4 to 12 weeks from first contact to completed installation. Simple measures like loft insulation are faster (4 to 6 weeks). Complex measures like solid wall insulation take longer (8 to 12 weeks) due to scaffolding and weather dependencies. Demand increases as the December 2026 deadline approaches, so applying early gives you the best chance of quick completion.

How much will you save?

Savings depend on which measures are installed. Typical annual savings on gas heating: loft insulation saves £135 to £355, cavity wall insulation saves £105 to £395, and solid wall insulation saves £200 to £590. Combined, a full package of measures can save £500 to £1,200 per year. Use our insulation calculator for your specific property.

What about the Great British Insulation Scheme?

The Great British Insulation Scheme (GBIS) was a separate programme with broader eligibility (council tax bands A to D, no benefits required). GBIS closed to new applications on January 31, 2026, with installations completing by March 31, 2026. ECO4 is now the primary route to free insulation and runs until December 2026.

Frequently asked questions

What is the ECO4 scheme?

A government scheme requiring large energy suppliers to fund free insulation and heating upgrades in eligible homes. Runs until December 2026. Check your eligibility.

Who qualifies for the ECO4 scheme?

Households receiving means-tested benefits with an EPC of D or below. Some households without benefits qualify through ECO4 Flex via their local council.

How do I apply for ECO4?

Contact your energy supplier directly (British Gas, EDF, E.ON, Scottish Power, Octopus, OVO, Shell Energy). They handle everything from eligibility to installation.

What does ECO4 cover?

Loft insulation, cavity wall insulation, solid wall insulation, floor insulation, heating upgrades, and draught-proofing. Depends on your home survey.

When does ECO4 end?

December 2026. Apply now to avoid missing out. Suppliers may reach their targets before the deadline.
